Machu Picchu, Peru



Machu Picchu was a major stop on the hippie trek in the 1960s. The Incans constructed this fascinating village. It is among the most popular tourist destinations in the world and is located in Peruâ€™s Andes Mountains. It is thought that this estate was established as the Pachacuti, the Inca monarch, estate because of its polished dry-stone walls and traditional Inca design. The Intihuatana, the Temple of the Sun, and the Room of the Three Windows are the three main buildings of this complex.
